The Story

From a tea-stall on the highway to your table

Himalayan Highway Restaurant began as a single steaming pot of momo served to bus drivers and porters on the Kathmandu–Pokhara route. Today we still cook the same way: fresh dough, sizzling woks, and spice blends carried down from the hills.

Why we're different

We source vegetables from nearby highland farms and hand-fold every momo to order. No shortcuts, no powders.
